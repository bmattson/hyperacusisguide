Middle Ear Anatomy

The middle ear is an air-filled cavity located between the eardrum (tympanic membrane) and the inner ear. It contains three tiny bones—the malleus, incus, and stapes—collectively known as the ossicles, which transmit and amplify sound vibrations from the eardrum to the cochlea. The middle ear also includes the Eustachian tube, which helps equalize pressure between the ear and the external environment, and several small muscles, including the tensor tympani and stapedius, which play roles in protecting the ear from loud sounds.
